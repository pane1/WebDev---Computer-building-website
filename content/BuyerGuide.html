Buyers Guide - RAM

RAM is essential for you do tasks both your CPU and GPU relies on the RAM so that they can operate, the more the there is the more efficent your computer will run while doing these tasks. That being said if you already have a motherboard or you have decide what motherboard you are going to get then you should take note on the specifications of that motherboard especially the supported RAM capacity, supported RAM frequency, the number of RAM slots, RAM slot channel configuration, and the type of RAM the motherboard supports, each will be a limiting factor on which RAM you should choose. The price of the RAM will vary from factors such as RAM amount, the number of RAM sticks in a kit, special features like RGB lighting, and RAM type.

When it comes to what you should buy for RAM the main factor is the amount that a kit provides and the frequency it has because other factors like the heat spread over the RAM does not play any role in any benefits. We recommend 16gb so that you can do most tasks on your computer from basic productivity tasks to high-end gaming, if that is not within your price range then the minimum you should be 8gb of RAM but doing multiple tasks at once will hinder your performance. For digital works we recommend 32gb because those take a bigger toll on the GPU and CPU for processing power and have more RAM will help with those tasks making it more efficent. After deciding the amount and you have reviewed the specifications of your motherboard you should consider the frequency and the channel configuration, if your motherboard does not support the frequency that you buy for the motherboard there will be problems. Channel configurations will decide on the kit of RAM sticks you buy because that is how the motherboard will use the RAM you provided by how the RAM sticks communicate with each other, if you have a quad-channel board then your motherboard will allow for all four sticks to communicate, if you have dual only two will communicate, some may have single channel slots, these can also be combined onto a single board that has four RAM slots but has two dual channels instead of quad-channel. This also plays a factor because the reason RAM is sold in kits is that the producer found that communication between these sticks are perfect but if you buy two single sticks their may be a possibility that their is interference, this most likely will not lead to problems but it's a possibility.

Buyers Guide - Storage Device

When deciding on a storage device first you should consider what your doing on the computer, productive tasks my require very little capacity on your storage device mainly due to the products of such tasks only requiring minimal space, gaming would require quite a large amount due to games becoming bigger and bigger every generation, and lastly for a workstation would vary in size depending on the industry. Also when it comes to storage capacity, as the size increase so does the price. There are currently three types of storage devices on the market each with their own benefits and weaknesses.

SSD:
The SSD is probably the fastest out the three types of storage devices, it can read and write data better than the other two but it has the highest cost per gb. The price also goes up when the SSD is using a M.2 port instead of SATA, this makes the data transfer speed even quicker. When it comes to longivity the SSD has the longest lifespan because it does not require physical parts and movement to operate. When considering what storage devices to get we recommend having at least a small SSD along with a HDD so that you can install your OS onto the SSD instead of the HDD so that you can have fast start-up when turning on your computer and still have plenty of storage space due to your HDD which we recommend to be at least 1 tb, if you plan on using only a SSD we recommend getting at least 512gb because if you are gaming often then you will quickly run out of room otherwise if your only doing productive tasks you should have ample storage space but will eventually run out.

Price Range: $40 CAD - $400 CAD
Model Recommendation: minimum - [128gb], average - [256gb, 512gb], premium - [1Tb+]

PROS

1.

2.

Durability: lasts longer than the other options

CONS

1.

2.

HDD:
This is a more old fashion storage device where your data is stored on a disk whereas the SSD stores data purely digital thus having the slowest speed out of the three storage devices, that being said that is in comparison to the other two devices in no means is a HDD slow. In terms of longivity of a HDD it has the worst lifespan at about 4-years because it is completely reliant on physical moving parts thus giving it ware and tare. A HDD is the best when in terms of budget because it has the best cost per gb out of the three. We recommend if your going planning on getting a HDD that you should pair it with a SDD of a decent size because you see a noticable difference in speed when it comes to start-up and will see a dip in performance as time passes. We do not recommend getting purely a HDD as storage because we feel that the lifespan is too short, that would mean that you would have to replace the storage device eventually which is a big hassle for the price point but we do understand if your budget is limited and we do think that HDD is the best priced then not having one at all.
